What is the relationship between mindfulness and self awareness?

I would say that their 'relationship' is being the same thing. Kinda like 'command line' and 'terminal', same thing but different names.

Try at least one of the self awareness practices from the chapter. What was your experience of it? How did you feel?

I already journal but recently I have been neglecting to do it on a daily or even weekly basis so I am pretty keen on getting back to it! I always find it really relaxing and I also find that somehow I just start instanly writing about something thats really bothering me which is suprising sometimes since I didn't realize until writng it down that it was effecting me so much. I've found this a really useful practice in terms of 'noticing and accepting' my own emotions.

What did you take away from the "My Emotions are Not Me" section?

It comes back to what I wrote about in my first week at EDA about the crazy asshole monkey in the back of your head. The 'monkey' is not nessisarily you talking. It's like sometimes I will shock myself when I think something really offencive or just in general something that don't line up with my values. I just think that my thoughts and feeling aren't always a reflection of me and what I believe. The 'monkey' just comes up with something crazy and you just have to think 'wtf?' to yourself and move on.
